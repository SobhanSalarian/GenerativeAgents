# Section 6 — Limitations, Future Work & Conclusion
---

## 6.1 Limitations

The findings of this thesis are established with strong internal validity within a controlled design; the following limitations bound their interpretation and are disclosed so that the empirical claims are read at the right altitude.

**Believability is confounded with architectural condition.** Composite believability rises with the cognitive architecture (baseline < memory < memory-plus-planning < full), so it is partly a proxy for condition. Consequently the aggregate association (H1) and the believability region (H2) risk restating, in believability terms, the simpler claim that richer architectures coordinate better. Two safeguards are applied. First, the principal associations are reported **within condition** as well as in aggregate, so that the believability–coordination relationship is assessed where architecture is held fixed. Second, the early-warning analysis (C4) is held to an explicit **within-condition test**: it is credited as predictive only to the extent that it forecasts collapse beyond what architectural condition alone explains, rather than acting as a disguised condition classifier. Where the within-condition signal is weak — and it may be, given the limited number of trials per condition — the relevant claim is reported as exploratory.

**The information-consensus task contributes binary, not fine-grained, evidence.** In the information-consensus scenario the three enriched conditions (memory, memory-plus-planning, full) each succeed in every trial at the same step, yielding effectively zero variance (an effective sample size of one per condition). The information-consensus task therefore supports a single, important, *binary* contrast — cascade failure in the baseline versus correct aggregation once memory is present — and it is on this contrast that the believability–validity decoupling result (C2/H5) most clearly rests. All fine-grained statistics (the threshold, the predictor regression, the early-warning model) draw on the commons-dilemma data, where genuine cross-trial variance exists. The thesis does not treat the forty information-consensus trials as forty independent fine-grained observations.

**A single agent architecture.** All results are obtained on one generative-agent architecture (Park et al., 2023). The instrument and experimental designs are architecture-agnostic by construction, but the empirical findings — including the focal-point reflection mechanism and the planning predictor — are not yet shown to transfer to other architectures (e.g. AutoGen, CAMEL, AgentVerse). Cross-architecture replication is identified as future work.

**Reflection saturation cuts two ways.** The full condition generates only two unique reflection strings across one hundred steps. The thesis reads this constructively, as evidence of a static focal-point norm (C3-ii). The same phenomenon is also a limitation: because each trial is relatively short and begins from a shared fork point rather than from a deeply accumulated reflective history, the design may *under*-exercise reflection, so the observed memory-plus-planning-to-full difference is best read as a **conservative lower bound** on reflection's potential contribution. It also means the categorical gain is mechanistically narrow — a small amount of normative content — which is precisely why the confirmatory injection experiment (P1) is worth running.

**No direct inter-agent communication in the commons dilemma.** In that task agents observe only the shared pool-depletion signal, never one another's text; coordination is therefore emergent through a single environmental channel rather than through negotiation or persuasion. This bounds the ecological breadth of the commons-dilemma results and, as discussed in §6.2, rules out certain causal manipulations (a surface-believability intervention has no channel through which to act).

**Planning's predictive dominance is observational, not causal.** Planning plausibility is the strongest micro-level predictor of coordination (H4), but in the agents' serial pipeline (observation → memory → planning → action) planning sits closest to the action, so its predictive dominance may partly reflect pipeline proximity rather than cognitive primacy. The thesis therefore reports H4 as an observational predictor against a memory-centred expectation, and does not claim a causal or cognitive-primacy result.

**Automated metrics are proxies, pending human validation.** The micro-level scores are computed automatically (embedding cosine similarity and a cross-provider LLM-as-judge). They are treated as proxies until blended with human ratings under reliability gating (Krippendorff's α ≥ 0.67); dimensions failing the reliability threshold revert to automated-only and are flagged. The internal sub-dimension weights of the composite (and the 0.30/0.25/0.25/0.20 top-level weights) are partly judgment calls; a sensitivity analysis of the weighting is reported, and the composite's robustness to reasonable reweighting is disclosed.

**LLM stochasticity and step-0 confabulation.** Outputs remain stochastic even under seed control, motivating repeated trials and transparent logging. Agents occasionally generate memory references at step 0 when no prior memories exist; these are scored low for memory coherence and retained in the data as a meaningful phenomenon rather than discarded.

**The believability region is within-study.** The ≈0.36–0.39 boundary is the highest believability value below which no trial in this study coordinated; it is reported as a within-study lower region, not a universal threshold, and its numeric value is specific to this instrument, architecture, and pair of tasks.

## 6.2 Future Work

Three experiments are designed and pre-registered as planned work. They are described here as designs; their outcomes are not pre-supposed and are not claimed anywhere in this thesis as results.

**P1 — Reflection content-versus-process injection (priority; low cost).** To confirm the focal-point interpretation of the reflection effect (C3-ii / H6), the two saturated reflection strings generated under the full condition will be injected *statically* into the matched memory-plus-planning architecture, compared against a tone-, length-, and position-matched placebo string. A substantial reproduction of the coordination gain would confirm that the benefit lies in the reusable *informational content* of reflection (a stable, shared coordination norm) rather than in the *dynamic process* of generating it. Because the gain is already known to be carried by only two strings, this experiment is inexpensive and functions as a controlled confirmation of an in-hand observation; it is the first follow-up to run.

**P2 — Planning-versus-memory manipulation (optional; confound disclosed).** A manipulation independently degrading planning content and memory content could in principle test the planning-predictor finding (H4) causally. However, because memory feeds planning in the serial pipeline, such a manipulation is confounded by pipeline proximity and cannot by itself establish cognitive primacy. It is retained only as an optional robustness ablation with this confound explicitly disclosed, not as a causal test, and is lower priority than P1.

**P3 — Believability × information-accuracy factorial (further future work).** A fully interventional factorial separating believability from decision-relevant information would test whether believability is itself causal. It is uninformative in the commons dilemma, where surface believability has no inter-agent channel through which to act, and only marginally informative in the present information-consensus task, where the enriched conditions show near-zero variance. It is therefore reserved for a future, communication-bearing task in which message believability can be manipulated independently of information accuracy and in which informative outcome variance is restored.

**Further directions.** Beyond the three experiments above, the natural extensions are: (i) **cross-architecture replication** of the instrument and findings on at least one non-Park architecture, to test the architecture-agnostic claim empirically; (ii) **longer horizons and accumulated reflective histories**, to relax the reflection-saturation ceiling and estimate reflection's contribution beyond a conservative lower bound; (iii) **a communication-bearing scenario** (e.g. an explicit negotiation or messaging channel) to study believability as transmitted persuasion rather than as a private trait; (iv) **external validation** of the human-blended scores at scale, including a second rater cohort; and (v) **additional coordination tasks** (e.g. the task-assignment scenario already stubbed) to test the generality of the threshold and the decoupling result beyond resource restraint and information aggregation.

## 6.3 Conclusion

This thesis set out to determine whether, and how, the individual believability of generative agents relates to valid emergent coordination in agent-based models — and to do so by measurement and diagnosis rather than by proposing a further validation framework. It contributes a working, human-validated dual-level instrument; a measurable demonstration that individual believability and collective validity *decouple*, such that believable agents can coordinate confidently on an invalid outcome; the finding that planning plausibility, not memory coherence, is the strongest micro-level predictor of coordination; and a mechanistic account of the categorical reflection effect as convergence on a static shared focal-point norm rather than continuous reasoning. It adds exploratory trace-based diagnostics for anticipating collapse, and designs a low-cost experiment to confirm the reflection mechanism. The central message for practitioners is cautionary and precise: individual believability is a useful *diagnostic signal* of valid coordination — it tracks, thresholds, and helps forecast collective validity — but it is necessary, not sufficient, and the conditions under which it fails can now be measured rather than merely warned against.
