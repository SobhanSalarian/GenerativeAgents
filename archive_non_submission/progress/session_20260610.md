### Methodological caveats (for thesis)

- Believability confounded with architectural condition — safeguard: within-condition tests
- IC contributes binary evidence only (zero variance C2–C4); disclose as limitation
- Single architecture (Park et al. 2023) — instrument is architecture-agnostic by design
- Reflection saturation = conservative lower bound on reflection's potential (100-step trials from shared fork)
- H4 planning dominance is observational — pipeline proximity confound; not a causal claim
- IC zero-variance: effective n=1 per condition for C2–C4; disclose explicitly
- Believability threshold is within-study, not universal
- LLM judge (Claude) used instead of GPT-4o-mini for judge-generator independence (Zheng et al. 2023)

## 3. Related work update plan — papers found (late 2025 – June 2026)

The related work was last reviewed 2026-05-24. The thesis reframing (2026-06-06) introduced new citations that need supporting literature. A search found 10 relevant new papers.

### High priority — add immediately

**[A] Zeng et al. (2026) "Too Human to Model: The Uncanny Valley of LLMs in Social Simulation"**
- arXiv: 2507.06310 | Published: *npj Complexity* 3, article 13, 2026 (peer-reviewed)
- *Where:* Lead citation for believability–validity gap in Introduction and §2.1 (GABM validation)
- *Why:* Peer-reviewed paper arguing surface believability actively worsens systemic validity — the strongest existing citation for the thesis's central claim. Five dilemmas: temporal mismatch, role drift, mode collapse, verbosity obscuring emergence, confusion of surface realism with systemic validity.

**[B] Zhao et al. (FAccT 2026) "Mechanism Plausibility in Generative Agent-Based Modeling"**
- arXiv: 2605.12824
- *Where:* §2.1 (ABM validation)
- *Why:* Introduces four-level Mechanism Plausibility Scale separating generative sufficiency (can the model reproduce a phenomenon?) from mechanistic plausibility (does it explain *how*?). Conceptual vocabulary for the dual-level framework.

**[C] Cho et al. (2025) "Herd Behavior: Investigating Peer Influence in LLM-based Multi-Agent Systems"**
- arXiv: 2505.21588
- *Where:* §2.3 (LLM-agent evaluation / multi-agent coordination)
- *Why:* Empirically demonstrates LLM herding driven by a confidence gap — the most direct test of Bikhchandani-style cascades in LLM agents. Directly supports the IC cascade result.

**[D] Jain & Krishnamurthy (2025) "Interacting Large Language Model Agents: Interpretable Models and Social Learning"**
- arXiv: 2411.01271
- *Where:* §2.3
- *Why:* Formally proves LLM agents form information cascades when public belief is strong. Mathematical grounding for the IC cascade claim.

**[E] Aharon et al. (Jan 2026) "Tacit Coordination of Large Language Models"**
- arXiv: 2601.22184
- *Where:* §2.3 or new §2.4 — focal-point / Schelling coordination paragraph
- *Why:* First large-scale test of LLMs in Schelling-point (tacit coordination) games. Directly operationalises focal-point theory for LLM agents — theoretical backbone for H6 reflection-saturation interpretation.

### Medium priority — add if space allows

**[F] Wu et al. (2026) "LLM-Based Social Simulations Require a Boundary"**
- arXiv: 2506.19806
- *Where:* §2.2 (generative agents in ABM)
- *Why:* Argues GABMs systematically under-report variance, producing homogeneous "average persona" outputs. Supports IC zero-variance disclosure and the thesis's transparency about effective sample sizes.

**[G] Yehudai et al. (May 2026) "Agentic CLEAR: Automating Multi-Level Evaluation of LLM Agents"**
- arXiv: 2605.22608
- *Where:* §2.4 (trace-based mining — the new section already planned)
- *Why:* Multi-level evaluation at system/trace/node granularity — methodological precedent for the thesis's own macro/micro measurement structure.

**[H] Zhong et al. (2025) "Disentangling the Drivers of LLM Social Conformity"**
- arXiv: 2508.14918
- *Where:* §2.3 alongside [C] and [D]
- *Why:* Mechanistic account of when LLM agents cascade (informational → normative influence as uncertainty rises). Theoretical depth for the IC cascade discussion.
