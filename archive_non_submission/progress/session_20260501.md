## 3. Human Evaluation Timeline

### How a packet works
Each packet = 1 agent at 1 sampled step. Contains: persona background, resource context, memories, and the decision (reasoning, memory reference, plan reference). Rater scores 4 Likert scales (1–5) + 1 binary (believable yes/no). Approximately 3–4 minutes per packet.

### Sampling recommendation
Do NOT rate all 80 trials (2,240 packets total — unworkable). Sample strategically:

| Approach | Trials sampled | Packets per rater | Time per rater |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum (1 trial/condition) | 4 | ~112 | ~7 hours |
| Stronger (2 trials/condition) | 8 | ~224 | ~14 hours |

**Recommendation:** Minimum sample is defensible for MRes. All raters rate the same packet set (required for Krippendorff's α). Budget 30 min training session before independent rating begins.

## 5. Persona Knowledge During the Experiment

### Answer: Yes — at every single step

The `_ask_agent()` method in `commons_dilemma.py` rebuilds the prompt from scratch on every call:

```
You are {persona.scratch.name}.

{persona.scratch.get_str_iss()}   ← full identity, traits, occupation, relationships
[condition-gated cognitive context]
Current situation: [resource state]
```

`get_str_iss()` is the Park et al. Identity/Stable-Status/Social string. It is injected at step 1, step 47, step 100 — every time.

### Why the LLM needs this re-injection
Each API call is stateless. The model has no memory of previous calls. Persona consistency is maintained entirely by re-injecting the identity string every step, not by the model retaining state.

### Implication for the thesis
- **Persona identity is a constant, not a variable.** The ablation is purely over cognitive architecture (memory, planning, reflection).
- **Behavioural consistency across steps is not automatic.** Even though the same persona text is injected each time, the LLM can still produce inconsistent decisions. That inconsistency is exactly what the `behavioural_consistency` metric detects — making it a genuine finding.
- **Thesis framing:** *"All conditions share a common persona foundation; conditions differ only in whether memory retrieval, planning, and reflection modules are active during decision-making."*
